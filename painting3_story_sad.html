Richard Godfrey Rivers

AN ALIEN IN AUSTRALIA

While the vibrant vermilion blooms of the 'Flame of the Forest' would have stood out against the dark greens of its home in the tropical forests of South-East Asia, in Australia, it was the showy lavender jacaranda that drew the most admiration.

The jacaranda of Rivers's painting survived until 1979, when it was blown over in a storm. A solitary specimen of the 'Flame of the Forest' still blooms in the Brisbane Botanic Gardens, as it has done since it was painted by Rivers.
